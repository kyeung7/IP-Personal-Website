Risk

A pygame driven implementation of the classical board game Risk. This game was created with collaboration
from Duncan Enzmann, Finn Dority, and Isaac Avery-Padberg. Users start at an animated menu where they
can choose to start the game, look up the controls, and quit. Up to 6 total players may play Risk at a time,
and solo players may play against a simple "Blitz AI." This computer player plays the game with the mindset
of attacking using their strongest force.

The same usual game rules apply. There are 6 continents on the map, composed of 42 total territories.
Depending on the number of players in the game, individuals will each start out with a set of territories
and troops. The game can be controlled via mouse and keyboard. The 'n' key proceeds to the next round. The
'h' key displays the help screen. Starting in the beginning, players enter a placement phase. Player turns
are divided into phases. Placement, attack, and movement. Players earn troops via bonuses and conquering
territories. Once they place troops on controlled territories, the players can choose to attack enemy territories.
When attacking, randomly generated die are simulated to calculate how successful the attack is. More attackers
and less defenders favor the attacking player. The game ends once a player has taken over all the territories.

Controls:
Right-Click/Left-Click (Mouse): Select/Deselect target territory
Up/Down Wheel (Mouse): Add/Subtract troops
'n' Key (Keyboard): Next round
'h' Key (Keyboard): Help screen
